Abstract

The El Niño Southern-Oscillation (ENSO) has long been known for its strong influence on global weather. Although localized to the Pacific Ocean, its effects can be seen across the globe, with notable effects around the Mississippi River Basin (MRB). In 2017, Muñoz and Dee found that the warm phase of ENSO preconditions the MRB to flood vulnerability [1]. Given the economic costs associated with natural disasters in this heavily populated region, understanding the connection between ENSO and flooding is of paramount importance—visualizing this connection is the focus of this paper.
